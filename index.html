INTERNSHIPS

Kusmi Tea

Discovery of IT tools and industrial automation

Computer Science & Automation

Orientis Gourmet is a French company specialized in tea sales, with a presence in over eighty boutiques across France, and also known internationally. It owns the brand Kusmi Tea, a leading high-end tea mark in France.

This one-month internship allowed me to explore various aspects of the company and contribute to several projects.

IT Department

During my internship, I observed and participated in the IT activities of Orientis Gourmet, including technical support and the implementation of tools such as the GLPI software for managing requests, as well as TeamViewer software for remote troubleshooting.

I also contributed to the deployment of new payment terminals in boutiques and the update of workstations in Prologis warehouses. This allowed me to discover the computer network and the importance of technological tools within the company.

Industrial Automation

At Orientis Gourmet, I was tasked with designing and programming an automation system for counting and grouping tea bags at the end of the production line.

I used a programmable logic controller (PLC) (TM221 by Schneider Electric) to manage electrical signals and program the features. Using EcoStruxure Machine software, I created a program in LADDER language, structured with steps and transitions, to control a conveyor belt motor. I then optimized the system by integrating a frequency inverter to control the speed of the conveyor belt and address issues related to bag grouping.

These experiences helped me develop my skills in electronics, programming, and industrial automation while also sparking reflection on the human implications of automation in the field of engineering.

Algorithms & Programming

As part of my academic journey, I undertook a series of diverse computer projects.

From solving algorithmic problems in Python to manipulating data in C, and even creating multiplayer applications, these projects showcase my progress in the field of computer science and my growing mastery of programming languages.

Python Optimization Challenge

In this collaborative project, we solved an algorithmic problem as a pair.

Using Python, we aimed to place the minimum number of "watchers" in a matrix in order to observe all the "targets" in both rows and columns. Our approach involved initially solving the problem and then optimizing it to reduce the total number of required watchers.

Through a day of intensive work, we explored various algorithmic strategies to achieve an optimal solution, highlighting our skills in problem-solving and optimization.

CSV Data Processing and Statistical Analysis in C

In this project, we worked in C to process and interpret data from Excel spreadsheets. Our goal was to generate meaningful statistics from the data and present them visually using a chart.

By developing these functionalities, we showcased our mastery of data manipulation and our ability to create efficient programs for statistical analysis.

C Prediction Dictionary

This C project involved creating a prediction dictionary that suggests words based on user input.

When the user began typing a character string, the application suggested likely words by referring to a pre-existing conversation dictionary. If the word wasn't found in this dictionary, we implemented logic to suggest common French words that completed the input.

This project highlighted our skills in text processing and creating data structures.

C Multithreaded Application: "Dactylo Race"

In this multiplayer application, we managed processes and threads to create an interactive gaming experience.

Players compete to accurately and quickly type out a displayed sentence. Using the concept of multithreading, we managed player registration, sentence display, and time counting. Once all players finished, the game displayed the podium and provided options to replay or exit.

This project demonstrated our ability to simultaneously manage multiple tasks in an interactive and competitive environment.

Robot design

Embedded Systems
Robot Project : Electronics and Programming in C

As part of my academic journey, I had the opportunity to immerse myself in a project involving the creation and testing of an electronic board to control a robot.

In pairs, we took charge of designing and testing the electronic board that served as the brain of the robot. This board was equipped with infrared sensors, a sonar, relays (motor control), and operating LEDs, with optimized power management. Our role was to ensure that each component interacted harmoniously to ensure smooth operation of the robot.

Using the embedded C programming language, we programmed the robot's behavior via the "PIC-18" microcontroller. The robot was programmed to perform a 180-degree scan in back-and-forth motion. Furthermore, once an object was detected within 1.50 meters, based on information from the infrared sensors, the robot would make a targeted advance toward the object. This behavior was designed to demonstrate the robot's ability to intelligently react to its environment.

This project allowed me to merge my knowledge of electronics, embedded programming, and system design to create a functional and intelligent robot. It was a hands-on experience that solidified my understanding of theoretical concepts and my ability to apply them in concrete projects.

Robotics

Elabroation of an exoskeleton arm

Exoskeleton Project: Design, Electronics, and Programming

During my journey in preparatory classes (CPGE), I had the opportunity to take part in a Personal and Guided Initiative Work (TIPEin French) project that immersed me into the world of control electronics and embedded programming.

This ambitious project aimed to design an exoskeleton prosthesis for an arm, with the objective of assisting the user in carrying loads at arm's length.

Mechanical Design and 3D

For the exoskeleton's structure, I chose an articulated brace as the main architecture. Using SolidWorks, I designed custom plastic parts to attach the motor at the elbow level.

Control Electronics

The next step involved setting up the electrical control systems. I used an H-bridge and a push button to manage the motor's movement. This part was crucial to ensure precise and safe control of the exoskeleton.

Embedded Programming

To bring the exoskeleton's movement to life, I programmed the motor's behavior using an Arduino UNO board. The programming was developed to ensure a smooth and natural movement of the exoskeleton.

This project provided me with a valuable opportunity to apply my knowledge in electronics and programming within a concrete and meaningful context.

It also strengthened my ability to tackle complex challenges and transform theoretical concepts into tangible achievements.
